RgA Rules — Recreational Golfers of America

RgA Rules are a unified set of relaxed rules better suited to speeding up play, eliminating unfortunate lies, and making the game a little bit easier and MUCH MORE FUN, while keeping things fair for gamblers.

The USGA Rules can be difficult to understand and apply — strict and procedural, leading to slow play. RgA Rules simplify penalty situations so players can keep moving.

Rules 1 – 5

Rule 1 — Breakfast Ball

Every player has the option to hit a second tee shot on their first hole of the day. Why ruin your entire day with a bad first shot?

  • If you choose to hit a second tee shot, you MUST play it — you may not play the better of the two.
  • The decision must be made while still on the tee. No going back.

Rule 2 — Improved Lies

"Lift, Clean, and Place" is permitted in the fairways, rough, woods, weeds, penalty areas, and bunkers (see Rule 3 for bunkers). You may place your ball on the best spot within a few inches of where it was at rest, as long as you don't change your cut of grass, move your ball from water or mud in penalty areas, or improve your line of sight or swing around trees, weeds, etc.

  • You may not touch your ball if it is on rocks, in water, or in mud in a penalty area.
  • You may not move any rock that would cause your ball to move.
  • You may move rocks impeding your line of sight or swing — even in a penalty area.
  • No penalty for accidentally making your ball move — replace it and play on.
  • You may not break branches or stomp weeds. If unplayable, take an unplayable lie, add a penalty, and play on.

Rule 2 eliminates cheating — "fluffing" when nobody is looking is the #1 way cheaters gain an advantage.

Rule 3 — Bunkers

"Rake and Place" in all bunkers, even "fried eggs." Rake a fresh spot (or scrape with your spikes) then place your ball where it was originally at rest.

  • If there's no fair spot due to water, erosion, etc., you may remove the ball from the bunker and drop equidistant (or further) from the hole.
  • A downhill lie to avoid water is not a fair spot — you may remove the ball from the bunker.

Rule 4 — Lost Balls and OB

If your ball is lost or unplayable in a penalty area (staked or un-staked: woods, weeds, water, deep grass, high fescue, etc.) or Out of Bounds — add a penalty stroke, then choose one of the following:

  • Re-hit from the original spot.
  • Drop within 2 club lengths of the point the ball entered the penalty area or OB.
  • Drop on a line keeping the entry point directly between your ball and the hole, going back as far as you want.
  • Take lateral relief by dropping on the opposite side of a RED penalty area, equidistant from the hole.
  • NEW — Drop on your "line of flight" somewhere between where the ball entered the penalty area and where you hit it. (If ball went past the pin, you may only go back 2 club lengths from the entry point.)

In all cases, it is where the ball ENTERED the area — not the total distance traveled. When dropping, you get relief from overhanging limbs, but not from trees further down the tree line.

Rule 4b — No Penalty Exceptions
  • Plugged Balls
  • Leaves
  • Thick Rough along fairway
  • Taken by Someone
  • Darkness

If your ball can't be found and the foursome agrees it DEFINITELY should have been found, drop in the believed area with no penalty. However, if it's possible it's in an intended penalty area, add a penalty stroke.

Rule 5 — Root Rule

You may take relief from roots that grow along the ground, but not the portion growing upward connected to the trunk. Nearest point of complete relief, no nearer the hole.

Relief is granted if the root affects your line of swing, line of sight, or stance — so nobody gets hurt. You don't necessarily get a clear shot. You get relief from roots, not from branches or trunks.

Rules 6 – 10

Rule 6 — Man Made Objects

If your ball lands near a man-made object (post, bench, OB fence, garbage can, cart path, etc.) you may take complete FREE relief. Determine the nearest point of complete relief — even if closer to the hole — place your ball there, and play on.

  • Relief applies if the object hinders your stance, swing, or line of sight.
  • Includes all flower beds and decorative areas where plants are at risk of serious damage from a swing.

Rule 7 — Accidental Ball Contact

No penalty for accidentally striking your ball or causing it to move during a practice stroke or at address. Since we play "Lift, Clean, and Place," quickly re-set your ball to your liking and play on.

Rule 8 — Hitting the Wrong Ball

No penalty for hitting the wrong ball once. Quickly replace it and proceed with the correct ball.

However, if you hit the wrong ball two consecutive shots, you must finish with it, count your strokes, and ADD A PENALTY. Be mindful of holding up play and fix errors as quickly as possible.

Rule 9 — Sharing Information

To speed up play, discussion of yardages and club selection between players is encouraged. GPS devices and laser range-finders (including slope models) are permitted and may be shared.

Rule 10 — Equipment

Any commercially available club (no maximum in your bag) or ball is acceptable. Players may swap out their ball at any time during play. However, you may not have separate balls for driving and putting, continually switching between the two.

Rules 11 – 15

Rule 11 — Touching Surfaces

No penalty for touching the surface of a bunker or penalty area with your club. You may touch your club to the sand or ground at address.

You may NOT:

  • Intentionally take practice swings that test the surface (accidents happen — intent to test is the breach).
  • Break branches or stomp weeds.
  • Excessively depress the sand behind the ball to make it easier to hit.
  • Move rocks in intended penalty areas that would cause your ball to move.

Any of the above = 1 stroke penalty.

Rule 12 — Don't Be a Jagoff

Players may not use obscure USGA rules for infractions that don't grossly affect play — playing out of order, not marking your ball on the green, accidentally hitting someone's equipment, etc. Just play on.

Rule 13 — Hole-In-One

Unlike regular golf tradition, the maker of a Hole-In-One is entitled to free drinks for the rest of the evening, paid for by the foursome or group (everyone on a golf trip or at an outing). Everyone should chip in to celebrate the achievement.

Rule 14 — RgA Shambles

Everyone in the group hits their tee shot. Then, each player may choose to play in from any of the tee shots — not everyone has to play from the same one.

  • Once you select a tee shot, place your ball within one club length of it and play to the hole per RgA rules.
  • No Breakfast Balls in Shambles.

Rule 15 — RgA Scrambles

Players should mark the spot of the chosen ball. Each player may place their ball within one club length of the marked spot, no closer to the hole.

  • Within one club length, you may change your cut of grass, move from behind a tree, from water, from a bunker, or from OB.
  • You only get one club length — no extra space for your stance.
  • On the putting green, you may only move your ball a maximum of 6", no closer to the hole.
  • No Breakfast Balls in Scrambles.
Speedy Play

RgA Tips for Speedy Play

  • Don't always mark your ball on the green — only mark when in someone's way or when cleaning.
  • Putt out whenever possible instead of always marking and waiting. Excessive marking really slows play.
  • Always know when you are next. Be ready to play when it's your turn.
  • Play "Ready Golf" — respect honors on the tee if the player is ready, but hit if they're not.
  • Watch your playing partners' shots and help search for lost balls.
  • Limit your time at the turn to food, drinks, supplies, and restrooms. Eat while playing.
  • Use smartphone apps, GPS devices, or laser range-finders for yardages.
  • Tee it forward — play from the appropriate tees for your skill level.
  • When sharing a cart, drop off your partner at their ball with their club, then go to your ball and hit. Meet up further down the fairway.
  • Learn to putt with the flag in — it really saves time.
Etiquette

RgA Guide to Golf Etiquette

Dress Code

  • No denim blue jeans. Wear khakis, slacks, or Bermuda-style shorts. Cargo shorts are fine at public courses.
  • Collared shirts are best, but nice collarless shirts (not t-shirts) are typically fine at public courses.
  • Sleeveless shirts are typically acceptable for women golfers only.
  • Soft spike golf shoes or sneakers are the norm. Most courses do not allow metal spikes.

Tee Times

  • Arrive 30 minutes before your tee time, check in at the pro shop, and be at the Starter 10 minutes early.
  • Arrive 45–60 minutes early if you plan to hit the range.

Speed of Play

  • Always keep up with the group ahead. Golf is much more enjoyable when played in 4 hours or less.
  • If unable to keep pace, allow faster groups to play through — but keep playing as they do.
Skins

RgA Rules for Skins

Single foursomes should play two separate games — a Front 9 Skins Game and a Back 9 Skins Game.

  • PAR or better to win a Skin. Two tie, all tie — the Skin is cancelled. No carry-overs.
  • Whoever earns the most Skins after 9 holes WINS and collects from the 2 players with the fewest Skins.
  • 2nd place (4 or 5 players) = No Pay. If 2 tie for 2nd, they split and pay half each. With 3 players, there is no 2nd Place No Pay.
  • Everyone tying for the fewest Skins pays the full amount — the Bonanza.

Foursome/fivesome: If 2 tie for 1st, they split. If 3 tie for 1st, the game is null.
Threesome: If 2 tie for 1st, the game is null.

Donnie Coultas Memorial Golf Tour

Played once a month, May through September, at courses all over Western Pennsylvania and Eastern Ohio. Dedicated to the memory of Donnie Coultas, Taylor Pawlish, and all of our friends — golfers or not — that left us too soon.

The Skins game format is used because Donnie and Taylor hated giving strokes. With a Main Game Skins game and a B Flight Skins game at each event, everyone can compete and handicap strokes don't determine the outcome. That's how they always wanted it.

Event Fee Breakdown

  • $35 event fee covers administration and entry into the Skins Game ($20/pp) and Closest-to-the-Pins on par 3s ($10/pp).
  • B Flight Players' $20 Skins fee is divided between the Main Game and the B Flight Skins Game.
  • B Flight = bottom 50% in HCP average playing at the event.
  • Only 24 spots at each event — reserve your spot early.

Membership

Regular Members ($150/season) are included in all aspects of the league:

  • Weekly matches
  • Pre-Season and Mid-Season Scramble events
  • Multiple weeks of Myrtle Beach points (Modified Stableford)
  • NEW 2025 — Multiple weeks of Low Net in each flight, paid during Scratch Matchplay Tournament

Qualifying

All new players (and players with fewer than 10 weeks the previous season) start as Guests. When flights are set, top Guests fill open spots based on ability — lowest Handicap Averages qualify, not who you know or when you signed up.

Players are encouraged to post as many 9-hole scores as possible before end of April. Email or text scores to Paul to have them added to the stat sheet.

Local Rules

Local Rules

  • #1 — You are assumed to be coming unless you call or TEXT to cancel. Possible $20 fine for no-call.
  • #2 — Everyone gets a Breakfast Ball on their first hole. If you hit it, you must play it. No CPs or Birdie Skins with a Breakfast Ball.
  • #3 — Matches tied after 9 holes = TIE. Playoff matches: proceed to next available hole, Sudden Death with no HCP. Exception: if handicap difference is 7+ strokes, the player continues to receive strokes on extra holes when warranted by the scorecard.
  • #4 — Triple Bogey is the max score to record for handicap purposes. You may score higher for match purposes.
  • #5 — Any match not played by start of playoffs = TIE.
  • #6 — The road that splits the golf course is NOT Out of Bounds.
  • #7 — Hole #5: nothing is OB on typical tee shots, even balls cutting the corner. NEW: staked OB now exists on the left side of the final third of the hole — play per Rule #8.
  • #8 — Holes #13 & #14: white staked OB. You may drop where the ball crossed the line (our rule), but cannot play a ball that is across the white line. Options: re-hit, drop within 2 club lengths of where it crossed, or drop on line-of-flight from the tee — all with 1 stroke penalty.
  • #9 — Hole #18: area around the Maintenance building is OB. Use the hedgeline as OB markers; lines extend to the lakes on both sides. No relief from hedges for balls under them but in play. Free relief from equipment parked outside the hedged area. NEW: Driving Range area is now OB — play per Rule #8.

Tee Information

  • White tees — Regular tees for all players
  • Hole #10 — Played as a Par 4. White tee = front of back tee area (260y). Gold tee = back of lower tees (225y).
  • Gold tees — Available to players who struggle to hit drives over 200 yards. Gold tee players play Gold tees on Par 3s and special positions on #10 (back of Blue tee), #12 (old lower tee box), and #13 (from very back edge of fairway).
Weather Policy

Official Weather Policy

You do not have to tee off if you don't want to. However, if you start a match, you must make every effort to finish it — even if you have to wait out weather and play in sloppy conditions until darkness. By joining the league, you are committed to staying until darkness if necessary.

Any unfinished match is SUSPENDED and must be completed before the following week's golf. Matches that are tied may be replayed from the beginning the next week.

The commissioner reserves the right to replay a match if a player leaves when it could have been finished, or to award a match to the leader if players are unable to meet before the 5:30pm start the next Tuesday.

Nightly Games

Birdie Skins — $10/player

  • Birdies on the same hole cancel each other.
  • $100 maximum value of a Skin.
  • No Birdies = money carries to next week.
  • Birdies from Breakfast Balls don't count.
  • All payouts rounded down to nearest $5, remainder rolls over.

Chip-In Game — $5/player

  • All chip-ins split the pot. No chip-ins = money carries to next week.
  • Chip-ins are anything holed from off the putting surface — par not needed.
  • $100 maximum value of a chip-in.
  • A 100+ yard hole-out wins the pot over all other chip-ins.
  • Multiple 100+ yard hole-outs split the pot regardless of distance.
  • All payouts rounded down to nearest $5, remainder rolls over.
